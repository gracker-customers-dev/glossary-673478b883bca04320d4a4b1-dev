Experiential learning

A learning process through which students develop knowledge and skills from direct experience.

Description

Experiential learning is a pedagogical approach that emphasizes the role of experience in the learning process. It is grounded in the idea that individuals learn best when they can engage directly with the material, allowing them to apply theoretical concepts in real-world situations. This approach can involve hands-on activities, simulations, internships, and reflective practices that promote critical thinking and problem-solving skills. In the context of teacher development resources, experiential learning enables educators to deepen their understanding of teaching strategies by participating in active learning environments. This not only enhances their pedagogical skills but also helps them to model effective learning experiences for their students. By integrating experiential learning into professional development, teachers can foster a more dynamic and engaging classroom atmosphere, ultimately leading to improved student outcomes and a greater sense of efficacy in their teaching practices.

Examples

  • Participating in workshops where teachers implement new teaching strategies in real classrooms.
  • Engaging in collaborative projects that require problem-solving and creative thinking among educators.

Additional Information

  • Experiential learning is based on the theories of educational theorists like David Kolb.
  • Reflection is a critical component of experiential learning, allowing teachers to analyze and improve their practices.
