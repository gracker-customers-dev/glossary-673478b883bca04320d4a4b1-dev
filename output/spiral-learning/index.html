Spiral learning

A pedagogical approach that revisits concepts over time, allowing for deeper understanding and mastery.

Description

Spiral learning is an educational framework where learners are exposed to the same concepts multiple times throughout their learning journey, each time at a deeper or more complex level. This method relies on the idea that revisiting material enhances retention and understanding. In the context of teacher development resources, spiral learning encourages educators to build upon previously acquired knowledge and skills, enabling them to make connections between different teaching concepts and practices. For instance, a teacher might first learn about basic classroom management techniques and then revisit this topic later, exploring more advanced strategies and their applications in various settings. This iterative process not only aids in reinforcing foundational knowledge but also fosters critical thinking and adaptability. In professional development, spiral learning can be implemented through workshops, peer collaborations, and reflective practices, ensuring that educators continuously evolve in their teaching methodologies.

Examples

  • A teacher revisits classroom management techniques in different workshops throughout the year.
  • Professional development sessions include revisiting instructional strategies with progressively complex scenarios.

Additional Information

  • Promotes long-term retention of knowledge and skills.
  • Encourages educators to adapt and refine their practices as they gain experience.
