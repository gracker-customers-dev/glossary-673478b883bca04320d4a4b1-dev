KQED

KQED is a public media organization that provides educational resources and support for teachers.

Description

KQED is one of the largest public media organizations in the United States, based in San Francisco, California. It offers a wealth of resources aimed at enhancing teacher development and improving student learning outcomes. KQED's education department focuses on providing innovative digital content, professional development opportunities, and community engagement initiatives. They create and curate educational materials that align with standards in various subjects, including science, history, and the arts. Through KQED Learn, educators can access a range of multimedia resources, such as videos, interactive lessons, and classroom activities designed to inspire critical thinking and creativity. KQED also offers workshops and training sessions for teachers to help them integrate media literacy and digital storytelling into their curricula. This support not only enriches the teaching experience but also empowers students to become engaged, informed citizens in a media-saturated world.

Examples

  • KQED Learn: A platform offering curated educational resources and lesson plans.
  • Professional development workshops focusing on media literacy and digital storytelling.

Additional Information

  • KQED is a member of the PBS system, providing a network of resources across the country.
  • The organization also produces original documentaries and programs that can be utilized in classrooms.
