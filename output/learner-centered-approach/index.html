Learner centered approach

An educational strategy that prioritizes the needs, experiences, and interests of students over traditional teaching methods.

Description

The learner-centered approach is an educational philosophy that focuses on the individual needs, preferences, and interests of students in the learning process. It shifts the role of the teacher from a primary source of knowledge to a facilitator or guide, encouraging students to take an active role in their own learning. This approach promotes critical thinking, collaboration, and self-directed learning, allowing learners to engage deeply with content and develop skills that are relevant to their lives. Educators are trained to create environments that foster inquiry, reflection, and personalized learning experiences. By utilizing diverse teaching strategies and tools, such as project-based learning, peer teaching, and technology integration, teachers can better accommodate the varied learning styles and paces of their students. Overall, the learner-centered approach aims to empower students, promoting greater ownership of their educational journeys and fostering a lifelong love for learning.

Examples

  • Project-based learning where students work on real-world problems.
  • Collaborative group work that encourages peer-to-peer teaching and discussion.

Additional Information

  • Emphasizes formative assessments to guide instruction and student growth.
  • Encourages the use of technology to personalize learning experiences.
