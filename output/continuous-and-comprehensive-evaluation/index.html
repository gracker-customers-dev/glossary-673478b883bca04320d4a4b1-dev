Continuous and comprehensive evaluation

A systematic approach to assess student learning through ongoing feedback and a variety of methods.

Description

Continuous and Comprehensive Evaluation (CCE) is an assessment framework that aims to evaluate students' progress and understanding throughout the academic year, rather than relying solely on terminal examinations. This approach emphasizes the holistic development of students by integrating both formative and summative assessments. Formative assessments occur regularly and provide immediate feedback to students and teachers, helping to identify strengths and areas for improvement. Summative assessments, on the other hand, evaluate students' learning at the end of a specific period. CCE encourages the use of diverse assessment techniques, such as quizzes, projects, class participation, and portfolios, which cater to different learning styles and provide a more rounded picture of student performance. By fostering a supportive learning environment, CCE aims to reduce exam stress and promote a love for learning, ultimately contributing to the overall development of students' skills and knowledge.

Examples

  • Conducting weekly quizzes to assess understanding of the material.
  • Using group projects to evaluate collaborative skills and creativity.

Additional Information

  • Promotes a student-centered learning approach.
  • Facilitates timely feedback for continuous improvement.
