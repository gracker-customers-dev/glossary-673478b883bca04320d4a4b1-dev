Narrative

A structured account of events or experiences, often used to convey a message or lesson in teacher development.

Description

In the context of teacher development resources, a narrative refers to the storytelling aspect of teaching and learning. Narratives can take the form of personal stories, case studies, or reflective accounts that illustrate particular teaching experiences, challenges, or successes. These narratives serve as powerful tools for professional growth, allowing educators to share their experiences with peers and reflect on their practices. By integrating narratives into teacher development, educators can engage in meaningful dialogue about pedagogical strategies, classroom management, and student interactions. Narratives help to humanize the teaching profession, providing relatable insights and fostering a sense of community among educators. They also promote critical thinking and analysis as teachers consider the implications of their stories for their own practice and for the broader educational context. Overall, narratives can enhance teacher training programs by facilitating deeper understanding and connection among educators.

Examples

  • A teacher shares a narrative about a successful project that engaged students in hands-on learning.
  • A reflective account of a challenging classroom experience and the strategies implemented to overcome it.

Additional Information

  • Narratives can be used in workshops to stimulate discussion and peer feedback.
  • They often highlight the importance of context and relationships in the teaching and learning process.
