Formative assessment

Ongoing assessments designed to monitor student learning and provide feedback.

Description

Formative assessment refers to a variety of assessment methods used by educators to evaluate student comprehension, learning needs, and academic progress during a lesson, unit, or course. Unlike summative assessments, which typically occur at the end of an instructional period and are used to evaluate student learning against a standard or benchmark, formative assessments are intended to be ongoing and provide immediate feedback to both students and teachers. This feedback can be used to adjust teaching strategies, improve learning outcomes, and foster a more responsive educational environment. Examples of formative assessment techniques include quizzes, classroom discussions, peer reviews, and observations. By integrating formative assessments into their teaching practices, educators can better understand student needs and tailor instruction to support individual learning paths, ultimately leading to enhanced student engagement and achievement.

Examples

  • Exit tickets where students summarize what they've learned at the end of a lesson
  • Interactive quizzes that provide instant feedback on student understanding

Additional Information

  • Formative assessments can be both formal and informal
  • They help teachers identify areas where students struggle and need more support
