Enquiry learning

An educational approach that encourages students to ask questions and explore subjects through inquiry and investigation.

Description

Enquiry learning is a student-centered pedagogical approach that emphasizes the role of questioning in the learning process. It encourages students to take an active role in their education by formulating questions, conducting investigations, and drawing conclusions based on their findings. This method fosters critical thinking, problem-solving, and independent thinking skills. Teachers act as facilitators, guiding students as they explore topics of interest and encouraging them to seek answers through research, experimentation, or discussion. Enquiry learning is particularly effective in promoting deeper understanding and retention of knowledge, as it engages students in meaningful exploration and connects learning to real-world contexts. By allowing students to pursue their own inquiries, they become more invested in their learning, which can lead to greater motivation and engagement in the classroom. This approach can be integrated across various subjects and adapted to different educational levels, making it a versatile tool in teacher development and curriculum design.

Examples

  • Students investigate the impact of pollution on local water sources by conducting experiments and interviews.
  • A science class conducts an enquiry project where students design their own experiments to test the effects of different variables on plant growth.

Additional Information

  • Enquiry learning supports the development of 21st-century skills, such as collaboration, creativity, and digital literacy.
  • This approach can be enhanced through the use of technology, including online research tools and collaborative platforms.
