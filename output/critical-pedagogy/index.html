Critical pedagogy

A teaching approach that encourages students to question and challenge power structures and inequalities.

Description

Critical pedagogy is an educational philosophy that emerged from the critical theory of the Frankfurt School and the work of educators like Paulo Freire. It emphasizes the role of education in promoting social justice and empowering students to think critically about their world. In the context of teacher development resources, critical pedagogy encourages educators to reflect on their own teaching practices, understand the cultural and social contexts of their students, and foster an environment where dialogue, critical thinking, and collaborative learning are at the forefront. This approach challenges traditional models of education that often prioritize rote memorization and passive learning. Instead, it advocates for a curriculum that is socially relevant and inclusive, allowing students to connect their learning to real-world issues. Teachers who embrace critical pedagogy aim to create a classroom culture where students feel valued and are encouraged to become active participants in their education and society, ultimately working towards transformative change.

Examples

  • Using culturally relevant materials to engage students in discussions about social issues.
  • Encouraging students to critically analyze media and literature for biases and perspectives.

Additional Information

  • Critical pedagogy can be applied across various subjects and grade levels.
  • It promotes collaborative learning environments where students learn from each other.
