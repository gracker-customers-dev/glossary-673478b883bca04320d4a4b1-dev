Learner-centred facilitation

An approach to teaching that prioritizes the needs, experiences, and learning styles of students.

Description

Learner-centred facilitation is an educational approach that emphasizes the active involvement of students in their own learning process. This method shifts the traditional teacher-centered paradigm, where the instructor is the primary source of knowledge, to a model where students are encouraged to take responsibility for their learning. In this context, the facilitator's role is to guide, support, and create an engaging learning environment that fosters collaboration, critical thinking, and problem-solving skills. Effective learner-centred facilitation involves understanding the diverse backgrounds and learning preferences of students, employing a variety of instructional strategies, and encouraging student input and participation. This approach not only enhances student motivation but also helps develop lifelong learning skills, as learners become more autonomous and engaged in their educational journey. Moreover, learner-centred facilitation can be adapted to various educational contexts, making it a versatile and impactful method for teacher development.

Examples

  • Group discussions where students share their perspectives and insights on a topic.
  • Project-based learning where students work collaboratively on real-world problems.

Additional Information

  • Incorporates diverse teaching methods to cater to different learning styles.
  • Encourages formative assessment and feedback to promote continuous improvement.
